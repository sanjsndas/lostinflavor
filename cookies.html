1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work, or work more efficiently, as well as to provide reporting information. Cookies allow a website to recognize a user's device and remember information about their preferences or past actions.

  1. Definition and Purpose:

    Cookies are essentially tiny digital identifiers that websites send to your browser. Their primary purpose is to store small pieces of information on your device, which can then be retrieved by the website on subsequent visits. This allows websites to "remember" you, personalize your experience, and make browsing more efficient by recalling preferences, login states, and other user-specific settings. They are fundamental to the modern web experience, enabling features like shopping carts and personalized content.

  2. How They Work:

    When you load a website, the server sends a cookie to your browser. Your browser stores this cookie. The next time you visit that same website, your browser sends the cookie back to the server. This exchange allows the server to recognize your device and retrieve any stored information related to your previous interactions. This seamless process occurs in the background, enhancing your user journey without requiring repetitive inputs.

  3. First-Party vs. Third-Party Cookies:

    First-party cookies are set by the website you are visiting (e.g., LOSTINFLAVOR.com). They are typically used to remember your preferences for that specific site. Third-party cookies are set by a domain other than the one you are visiting, often by advertisers or analytics providers, and are used for tracking across different websites. Understanding this distinction helps you manage your privacy settings more effectively.

  4. Session vs. Persistent Cookies:

    Session cookies are temporary and are erased when you close your web browser. They are used to track your activities during a single browsing session. Persistent cookies, on the other hand, remain on your device for a set period or until you manually delete them. These are used to remember your preferences over longer periods, such as keeping you logged in or remembering your language choice.

  5. Cookie Data Storage:

    Cookies typically store non-personally identifiable information, such as a unique ID, site settings, or browsing history. While they don't directly contain your name or email, they can be linked to other data to build a profile. It's important to understand what data is being stored and how it might be used in conjunction with other information.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

LOSTINFLAVOR utilizes various categories of cookies to provide and improve our services.

  1. Strictly Necessary Cookies:

    These cookies are essential for you to browse the Site and use its features, such as accessing secure areas of the website. Without these cookies, services like user login, shopping carts, or e-billing cannot be provided. They are fundamental to the basic operation of LOSTINFLAVOR.com and cannot be turned off in our systems.

  2. Performance Cookies:

    These cookies collect information about how visitors use a website, for instance, which pages visitors go to most often, and if they get error messages from web pages. These cookies don't collect information that identifies a visitor. All information these cookies collect is aggregated and therefore anonymous. It is only used to improve how a website works.

  3. Functionality Cookies:

    Functionality cookies allow the website to remember choices you make (such as your user name, language, or the region you are in) and provide enhanced, more personal features. For instance, a website may be able to provide you with local weather reports or traffic news by storing in a cookie the region in which you are currently located. They can also be used to remember changes you have made to text size, fonts, and other parts of web pages that you can customize.

  4. Targeting/Advertising Cookies:

    These cookies are used to deliver advertisements more relevant to you and your interests. They are also used to limit the number of times you see an advertisement as well as help measure the effectiveness of the advertising campaign. They are usually placed by advertising networks with the website operatorâ€™s permission. They remember that you have visited a website and this information is shared with other organizations such as advertisers.

  5. Analytics Cookies:

    Specifically, these are a subset of performance cookies used to gather data on how users interact with the Site. This includes tracking page views, session duration, referral sources, and bounce rates. Tools like Google Analytics fall into this category, providing us with insights to optimize content and user flow, ultimately enhancing your browsing experience.

  6. Social Media Cookies:

    These cookies are set by social media services that we have added to the Site to enable you to share our content with your friends and networks. They are capable of tracking your browser across other sites and building up a profile of your interests. This may impact the content and messages you see on other websites you visit.

  7. Security Cookies:

    These cookies are vital for the security of our Site. They help to detect and prevent malicious activities, such as brute-force attacks, unauthorized access attempts, and other forms of cyber threats. By identifying suspicious patterns, these cookies contribute to a safer browsing environment for all LOSTINFLAVOR users.

  8. Preference Cookies:

    These cookies are used to store your preferences and settings on the Site, such as your chosen language, currency, or unit of measurement for recipes. They ensure that your customized settings are remembered across sessions, providing a consistent and convenient user experience without requiring repetitive adjustments.

  9. Authentication Cookies:

    When you log into your LOSTINFLAVOR account, authentication cookies are used to verify your identity and keep you logged in as you navigate through the Site. This eliminates the need to re-enter your credentials on every page, streamlining your access to personalized features and content.

  10. Cookie Consent Cookies:

    Ironically, a cookie is used to remember your cookie consent preferences. Once you accept or decline cookies via our banner, a specific cookie is set to store this choice, preventing the banner from reappearing on every subsequent visit for a specified period. This ensures a smoother and less intrusive user experience.

5. Your Choices Regarding Cookies

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. You can exercise your cookie preferences by adjusting your browser settings.

  1. Browser Settings:

    Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences. You can typically configure your browser to accept all cookies, reject all cookies, or notify you when a cookie is being sent. The exact steps vary depending on your browser (e.g., Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge).

  2. Opt-Out Tools:

    You can opt-out of interest-based advertising from many third-party advertising networks through industry opt-out tools such as the Network Advertising Initiative (NAI) Opt-Out Tool or the Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA) Opt-Out Tool. These tools allow you to manage your preferences across multiple advertising providers.

  3. Impact of Disabling Cookies:

    Please note that if you choose to disable or reject cookies, some parts of our Site may not function properly or may become inaccessible. For example, you might not be able to log in, save recipes, or experience personalized content. Disabling certain cookies can significantly impact your overall browsing experience.

  4. Specific Cookie Management:

    While global browser settings are an option, some users prefer to manage specific types of cookies. For instance, you might choose to block third-party advertising cookies while still allowing essential and functionality cookies to ensure the Site operates correctly. This granular control offers more flexibility.

  5. Clearing Cookies:

    You can also delete cookies that have already been placed on your device. This can be done through your browser settings. Clearing cookies will remove any stored preferences and login information, requiring you to re-enter them on subsequent visits to websites.

  6. Consent Withdrawal:

    If you have previously consented to the use of cookies and wish to withdraw your consent, you can do so by adjusting your browser settings to block or delete cookies. Note that this will not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

  7. Mobile Device Settings:

    For mobile devices, you may have options to limit ad tracking in your device settings. For example, on iOS devices, you can enable "Limit Ad Tracking," and on Android devices, you can enable "Opt out of Ads Personalization." These settings can help control how your mobile activity is used for advertising.

  8. Browser Extensions:

    Various browser extensions are available that offer enhanced cookie management features, allowing you to block, manage, or delete cookies more easily. These tools can provide an additional layer of control over your online privacy.

  9. Regular Review:

    We recommend regularly reviewing your browser's cookie settings and periodically clearing your cookies to maintain control over your privacy. This proactive approach ensures that your preferences are consistently applied across your browsing activities.

  10. Further Information:

    For more detailed information about cookies and how to manage them, you can visit external resources such as allaboutcookies.org or your online browser's help section. These resources provide comprehensive guides on various aspects of cookie management.
